Shopping for okonomiyaki ingredients in NYC

Your best bet for one-stop shopping is Sunrise Mart, a Japanese grocery with three locations in NYC (Midtown, Soho, and the East Village). There’s also a Tokyo Mart in Chinatown and a bevy of others I can’t vouch for if you just poke around Yelp for “Japanese grocery” (the 50’s are rife with them, I think).

Your shopping list probably looks like this:

  • Nagaimo, a.k.a. that weird sticky yam
  • Hon Dashi, the seafood-y stock that comes as lil’ pellets
  • Bonito flakes, shreds of fermented fish (…that sounds terrible, but you know what I mean)
  • Aonori, sprinkle-able seawood - or just nori sheets you can crush up
  • Egg noodles, which is more or less spaghetti that has already been cooked

I get irritated every time I have to buy nagaimo from Sunrise Mart in 3” lengths, but you can find it all over Chinatown and maybe other markets for $cheap.

Making okonomiyaki

You have the handout, sure, but videos are your best friend! Or a dog, I guess? Or a dog in a video, teaching you okonomiyaki-making? If you’re unfamiliar with Cooking with Dog, let’s take a wild ride together.

I believe this one was filmed with a half-broken videocamera in the late 1700’s, but it does a great job showcasing the sheer size of hiroshima-style okonomiyaki.

As a point of comparison, look at this laughably tiny Hiroshimayaki. She usually has great recipes, but this one isn’t even worth thinking about!!!

It’s gorgeous, yes. But the two most key ingredients in hiroshima-style okonomiyaki is the ability to impress your friends, along with constant and pressing danger from a very high stack of cabbage.
